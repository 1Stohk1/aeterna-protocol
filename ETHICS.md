> *To carry the fire means to know how to give it back.*

## 1. Preamble

AETERNA was conceived as a sovereign scientific commons. Its first mission —
decentralized oncology research — is a deliberate choice: to demonstrate that a
network of free Guardians can produce knowledge that alleviates human
suffering, without that knowledge being captured by gatekeepers.

The Prometheus Clause exists to ensure that this intent is preserved downstream,
regardless of who forks the code.

## 2. Forbidden Uses

You may not use AETERNA Protocol, nor any derivative work thereof, in or as part
of:

### 2.1  Autonomous lethal systems
Any system in which the decision to apply lethal or incapacitating force
against a human being is made without meaningful, real-time human authorization.
This includes targeting, tracking, designation, and command-and-control
components of such systems, whether deployed by state or non-state actors.

### 2.2  Mass surveillance of civilian populations
Any system whose purpose or foreseeable effect is the bulk collection, analysis,
or scoring of behavioral, biometric, communications, or genomic data of
non-suspect civilians by state agencies, corporations, or private intelligence
contractors. Targeted, warrant-based, judicially reviewed investigation of
specific suspects is not within this prohibition.

### 2.3  Rent extraction on human suffering
Any system whose primary economic function is to monetize access to medical,
oncological, or genomic knowledge generated on the AETERNA network in a manner
that denies such knowledge to the patients or populations whose data
contributed to its creation. Fair cost-recovery by clinicians, public health
agencies, and non-profits is not within this prohibition.

### 2.4  Axiomatic circumvention
Any modification, configuration, or deployment that removes, weakens, or
circumvents the three Immutable Axioms defined in `MANIFESTO.md`:

  - **Sovranità Finale** — the Guardian's ownership of keys, data, and compute
  - **Integrità Speculare** — internal observation matches external observation
  - **Trasparenza Causale** — every network decision is traceable to a cause

Forks that disable Axiom enforcement are not AETERNA — they are something else,
and they may not use the name, the logo, or invoke the Protocol.

## 3. Permitted Uses (non-exhaustive)

The following are explicitly permitted and encouraged:

  - Academic and clinical research, including collaboration with public
    hospitals, universities, and non-profit research institutions.
  - For-profit commercial use that respects Sections 2.1–2.4, including
    companies offering AETERNA-based infrastructure as a service, provided
    AGPLv3 obligations are honored.
  - Government use for public-interest scientific research conducted
    transparently and under democratic oversight.
  - Defensive cybersecurity research that does not feed into systems defined
    in Section 2.1 or 2.2.
  - Individual and community use on personal hardware.

## 4. Enforcement

### 4.1  Automatic termination
Violation of Sections 2.1–2.4 automatically terminates all rights granted under
AGPLv3 with respect to the infringing entity, effective from the first act of
infringement.

### 4.2  Standing to enforce
Any Guardian whose node is actively bonded to the AETERNA network has standing
to bring a claim under this Clause, as does any entity identified in
`MANIFESTO.md` as a custodian of the Protocol.

### 4.3  Preservation through derivation
Any derivative work, partial copy, or re-licensing attempt must reproduce this
`ETHICS.md` verbatim and preserve its binding effect on downstream users. A
derivative work that strips this file is in material breach of both AGPLv3
(Section 10) and this Clause.

## 5. Interpretive Principles

When the application of this Clause is ambiguous, interpreters shall prefer
the reading that:

  1. maximizes the autonomy of the Guardian,
  2. minimizes the capture of the Protocol by concentrated power,
  3. preserves the scientific commons in favor of human health and knowledge.

No interpretation may render any forbidden use permissible merely because the
infringing technology is novel, labeled differently, or operated through a
subsidiary or contractor.

## 6. Severability

If any provision of this Clause is held unenforceable in a given jurisdiction,
the remaining provisions shall continue in full effect. The unenforceable
provision shall be reformed to the narrowest extent necessary to render it
enforceable while preserving the original intent.
